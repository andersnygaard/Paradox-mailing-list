Main Entry: 1scarf 
Pronunciation: 'sk=E4rf
Function: noun
Inflected Form(s): plural scarfs
Etymology: Middle English skarf, probably of Scandinavian origin; akin to Old Norse skarfr scarf
Date: 15th century
1 : either of the chamfered or cutaway ends that fit together to form a scarf joint
2 : a joint made by chamfering, halving, or notching two pieces to correspond and lapping and bolting them -- called also scarf joint
